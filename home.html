Basketball Rules

Registration fee - Boys: Rs.1200 , Girls: Rs.1000

MEN & WOMEN


Rules:

1) OLD DIMENSIONS
2) The game will be of 4 quarters of 10 minutes each with 2 minutes break after a quarter and a 5 minutes break at half-time.
3) Teams should report to the basketball court half an hour before their match.
4) Use of abusive or foul language during the play can lead to technical fouls or expulsion depending on the referee.
5) If the score is tied at the end of playing time in the fourth period ,the game shall continue with as many time periods of five minutes as is necessary to break the tie.
6) An interval of play ends:
      a. At the beginning of the first period when the ball leaves the hand of the referee on the jump ball.
      b. At the beginning of all other periods when the ball is at the disposal of the player taking the throw-in.
7) A jump ball situation occurs when:
      a. A held ball is called.
      b. The ball goes out-of-bounds and the officials are in doubt of the player who touched the ball last.
      c. A double free-throw violation occurs during an unsuccessful last or only free throw.
      d. A live ball lodges between the ring and the backboard (except during free throws).
      e. The ball becomes dead when neither team had control over the ball now was entitled to the ball.
      f. After the cancellation of equal penalties against both the teams, there are no foul penalties remaining for administration and neither team had control over the ball was entitled to the ball before the first foul of violation.
      g. All other periods other than the first quarter are about to begin.
8) A player shall not run with the ball, deliberately kick or block the ball with any part of leg or strike it with the fist.
9) The player taking throw-in shall not
      a. Take more than five seconds to throw the ball.
      b. Step into the playing court while having the ball in his hand(s).
      c. Cause the ball to touch out-of-bounds, after it has been released on throwing.
      d. touch the ball on the playing court before it has touched another player.
      e. Cause the ball to enter the basket directly.
10) During the throw-in, the other players shall not
      a. Have any part of their bodies over the boundary line before the ball has been thrown-in across the boundary line.
      b. Be closer than one (1) meter to the player taking the throw-in when the throw-in has less than two(2) meters between the boundary line and any out-of bounds obstructions.
11) Time-out:
Each-out shall last one (1) minute
      A time-out may be granted during a time-out opportunity
      A time-out opportunity begins when:
      # for both teams, the ball becomes dead, the game clock is stopped and the official has ended his communication with the scorer’s table.
      # for both the teams, the ball becomes dead following a successful last and only successful or only free throw.
      # for the non-scoring team, a field goal is scored.
      A time-out opportunity ends when the ball is at the disposal of a player for a first or only free throw or for a throw-in
Two (2) timeouts may be granted to each team at any time during first half: three (3) at any time during second half: and one (1) extra during extra time
Unused timeouts may not be carried out to the next half or extra period
1) Game lost by forfeit : A team shall lose a game by forfeit if
A. Fifteen minutes (15) after the scheduled time ,if the team is not present or is unable to field five(5) players to play
b. Its actions prevent the game to be played
c. It refuses to play after being instructed to do so by the referee
13) Player out of bounds and ball out of bounds:
The ball is caused to go out of-of-bounds by the last player to touch or be touched by the ball before is goes out of-of-bounds, even if the ball then goes out-of-bounds by touching something other than a player
If the ball is out-of-bounds because of touching or being touched by a player who is on or outside the boundary line, this player causes the ball to go out-of-bounds
If the player goes out-of-bounds or to his backcourt during a held ball, a jump ball situation occurs
2) A player shall not dribble a second time after his first dribble has ended unless between the two(2) dribbles he has lost control of a live ball on the playing court because of:
a. A shot for a field goal
b. A touch of the ball by the opponent
c. A pass or fumble that has touched or been touched
If one foot is touching the floor, that foot becomes the pivot foot
If both the feet are off the floor and the player lands on both the foot simultaneously, the moment one foot is lifted, then the other foot becomes the pivot foot
If both feet are off the floor and the player lands on one foot, then that foot becomes the pivot foot. If a player jumps off that foot and lands on both the feet simultaneously, then neither foot is a pivot foot
Progressing with the ball for a player who has established a pivot foot while having the control over the ball on the play g court :
a. While standing with both the foot on the floor
# To start a dribble, the pivot foot may not be lifted before the ball is released from the hand(s)
# To pass or shoot for a field goal, the player may jump off the pivot foot, but neither of the foot may be returned to the floor before the ball is released from the hand(s)
15) The layer shall not remain in the opponents’ restricted area for more than 3 seconds while his team is in control of a live ball in the frontcourt and the game clock is running
16) A closely guarded player must pass, shoot, or dribble within five seconds
17) Whenever:
# A player gains control of a live ball on the playing court
# On a throw-in, the ball touches or is legally touches by any player on the playing court and the team of that player taking the throw remains in control of the ball, that team must attempt for a shot for a field goal within 24 seconds
18) To constitute a shot for a field goal within 24 seconds
# The ball must leave the player’s hand(s) within 24 seconds second clock signal sounds, and
# After the ball has left the player’s hand(s), the ball must touch the ring or enter the basket
# A player whose team is in control of a live ball in their front court may not cause the ball to be illegally returned to his backcourt
19) In event of all the 3 teams in a pool winning a match in the league, then the difference in the points will be considered to declare the result
20) Other FIBA rules are applicable

Chess

Registration fee: Rs.500 per team

Chess Rules:

Limit of number of players:

Classical: 5(4+1 reserve)
Lightning: 5(4+1 reserve)

General chess rules:
1) All FIDE chess rules apply as on January 1st ,2010.
2) Touch to move: If a player touches a piece, he/she must move it. It also requires him/her to capture opponents' piece if he/she touches it. This rule only applies if the player can make a legal move with the piece touched. If a player accidentally brushes a piece he/she is not required to move it. If a player touches a piece by non-playing area (like elbow), then he is not required to move the piece. If a piece is awkwardly placed then you can adjust it; simply say “I adjust” before touching the piece to make it clear to the opponent that you do not intend to move it
3) Clock rules: Opponents must press the clock with the same hand that he moves the pieces. If not he/she may be penalized one minute, which will be added to the opponent’s time. The chess clock will be placed on black’s right hand side.
4) Illegal move: If one player makes an illegal move, while playing on the clock, two minutes will be added to the opponent’s time. The player also must move the piece that he touched, if legal.
5) Winning on time: Whichever player’s flag falls first, loses the game (on time). To claim a win on time, stop both clocks-claimants flag must be still standing and he/she must have winning material. If both the flags are down, then the game is drawn.
6) Rules of draw:
Draw by offer: A proposal of draw must be made after a move and before pressing the clock. (make the offer of draw while the claimant’s clock is running) the opponent might accept it or reject it. Any opponent who makes an improper draw offer may be penalized one minute added to the opponent’s time
Draw by threefold repetition: The claimant must present a score sheet demonstrating that the same position: A) is about to occur or B) has occurred with the same player to move. It must be the claimant’s move to make such a claim. If the claimant makes a move and stop the clock, the claim will be considered invalid.
50 move rule: If a player shows that (with moves written on score sheet) 50 moves have been made without a pawn being moved or any capture made, then he/she may claim the game a draw. Both the clocks should be stopped before claiming for a draw
Stalemate: If the player on turn has no legal move but it is not on check, this is stalemate and the game is a draw
Impossibility of checkmate: If the position arises in which neither of the players could possibly give a checkmate by a series of legal moves, the game is a draw. This is usually because there is insufficient material left.
TOUNAMENT RULES AND INFORMATION:
1) The board order of players should be given beforehand to the committee. It cannot be changed except as per the substitution rule
2) In case of a 2-2 draw between the playing teams in critical rounds, three blitz matches will be played between the best players of each team. The captains of both teams will report the names of the best player to the organizer confidentially. In case of further draw, a final Armageddon match will be held
3) Rule for substitution: “Roll over substitution”
The boards lower to the board on which substitution is made would slide one board till the board of substitution
Example 1: Assume following the board order A-1,B-2,C-3,D-4. Substitute player E. In that case, the player A (on board 1) is to be substituted. New board order would be B-1, C-2, D-3, E-4. (Non-playing A)
Example 2: Assume the board order A-1, B-2, C-3,D-4. Substitute player E. In case player C is to be substituted (on board 3), then the new board order would be A-1,B-2,D-3,E-4.
Violation of these rules would lead to ‘0-4’ loss to the violating team for that round.
4) Contact the organizers to clear your confusion/dispute. If a player has a disagreement he/she should stop the clock, contact an arbiter and ask for a solution. The chess clock should be stopped before calling an organizer
5) All participating teams are requested to bring ‘chess clocks’. The player is required to know how to use a clock. A player may ask about its operation before the clocks are applied.
6) It is expected that players record the moves. (sheet will be provided) It may help to resolve some disputes. At the end of each game, a signed copy (by both players) of result sheet should be handed over to the organizers. The moves should be written in standard chess notation.
7) NO PROMPTING IS ALLOWED. (even if violation of any rule is noticed by spectators) Any person found guilty of prompting will be disqualified and strict action would be taken against the team to which he/she belongs.
8) A player or his/her team can be disqualified if his/her conduct in the tournament hall is not found satisfactory
9) After the game is over, the players should leave the table immediately, However, the captains of the playing teams can stay inside the playing arena even if his/her match is over.
10) Walkover would be given to the player if his/her opponent doesn’t turn up in 30 minutes (for classical chess) after the round has started. Any player absent from the playing room or board for more than fifteen minutes without permission from the committee may have his/her game forfeited due to unsportsmanlike conduct
11) Please switch off your mobiles of keep it in silent mode. Everyone present in the hall is expected to maintain silence. Violating team will likely be subject to a penalty, and even may have to forfeit their game
12) Parents, coaches, managers are not allowed inside the playing arena.
RULES SPECIFIC TO LIGHTNING CHESS:
1) Time of game will be 5 minutes to each player per game
2) Illegal move pointed out by the opponent immediately results in a loss
3) Players don’t need to point out a check to the opponent
RULES SPECIFIC TO ANTI CHESS
The rules are those for standard chess, except for the following special rules:
1) Capturing is compulsory.
2) When more than one capture is available, the player may choose.
3) The king has no royal power and accordingly:
# It may be captured like any other piece.
#There is no check or checkmate.
#There is no castling.
#A pawn may promote also to king.
4) Stalemate is a win for stalemated player.
A player wins by losing all his pieces, or being stalemated. Apart from move repetition, draw by agreement, and the fifty-move rule, the game is also drawn when a win is impossible (such as if a dark-squared bishop and a light –squared bishop are the only pieces remaining).

Carrom rules

Registration fee: Rs.500 per team

Rules:
1) Position adopted for sitting by a player before taking his turn to strike may be changed at any time provided the chair or stool on which he is sitting in not lifted, moved, or disturbed during his turn of play.
2) During the board no part of the body of the player except the playing arm shall touch the carom board, stand or table on which the board placed.
3) No part of the body, except the hand of the player shall go beyond the imaginary lines of arrows. Hand means the portion of the playing hand from the fingers to the wrist.
4) Any hand might be used to play.
5) A) While making a stroke, the elbow of the playing hand shall not come within the playing surface nor shall extend beyond the imaginary lines of the arrows.
B) The hand may however cross the cross the arrow.
6) A) While making a strike taking support of the stand or the table of the C/B and/or keeping the legs on the rim of the stand, by the player is not permissible.
B) However, hands may rest on his body and legs may rest on the rim of the stool or the chair, on which he sits.
7) A) Break is not considered to be made if no C/m is touched by the striker in its usual run or jumping out. In that case, a maximum of two more chances shall be allowed.
B) If after permissible number of chances, no C/m is touched, the right to break shall be lost and the turn to play shall be passed on to the opponent who shall have black C/m.

Power Lifting Rules:

Rules:

1.The competition shall be conducted under the International Power Lifting Federation Rules as adopted by the Indian Powerlifting Federation, unless otherwise modified.
2.The competition will comprise of 3 lifts-Bench Press, Squat & Dead Lift performed in the same order.
3.Every Lifter will get 3 lift attempts each for bench press, squat & dead lift.
4.The progression after every successful attempts for the same athlete must be a minimum of 1 kilogram.
5.Winner will be judged by the sum of heaviest lifts performed successfully for each kind of lift.

Fouls:-

1.SQUAT
Failure to bend the knees and lover the body until the top surface of the legs at the hip joints are lower than top of the knees.
Failure to assume the upright position with the knees locked at the commencement and completion of the lift. Any downward moment of the bar during the lift. Changing the position of the bar across the shoulder after commencement of the lift. Any downward movement of the bar on the back more than diameter/thickness of the bar below the lifter’s chosen starting position.
Stepping backward or forward although lateral movement of the foot and rocking the feet between the ball and heel is permitted.
2.Bench Press
Bar is not lowered to chest i.e. not reaching the chest or is touching the abdominal area.
Any pronounced or exaggerated uneven extension of arms during the lift. Any downward movement of the whole of the bar in the course of being pressed out. Failure to press the bar to fullest extension of the arms at the completion of the lift.
Heaving, or sinking the bar after it has been motionless on the chest, in such a way as to aid the lifter.
3.Dead lift
Failure to lock the knees straight at the completion of the lift. Failure to stand erect with the shoulder back.
Any downward movement of the bar before it reaches the final position. If the bar settles as the shoulder come back this should not be reason to disqualify the lift. Supporting the bar on the thighs during the performance of the lift. If the bar edges up the thighs is not supported, this is not reason for disqualification.
Lowering the bar before receiving the referee signal.

Throwball Rules

Registration fee: Rs.1000

Rules :
1.Each team should consist of Seven (7) players at time of play.
2.The team shall consist of 7 active players and substitutes.
3.The match should be played for best of 3 sets for 25 points in each set with rally score.
4.Any ball to be released should be released from above the shoulder/shoulder-line only.
5.The ball has to be caught with both the hands and returned in one hand only.
6.Service ball should not touch the net.
7.Double Touch is not allowed for the service ball.
8.The ball should be served after the whistle and within 5 seconds.
9.Serve the ball only from the service-zone & without crossing the end line.
10.Any ball falling on the box line or in the dead line is a foul.
11.Any ball after catching (during rally) should be released within 3 seconds.
12.During the rally, ball can touch the net.
13.Body touch, during catching or releasing the ball should not touch any part of the body except the palm.
14.Dubs while catching, the ball should be caught simultaneously with both the hands.
15.Two players cannot catch the ball simultaneously.
16.Any ball (service or rally) falling on the side-line or the end-line is a good ball.
17.The players should stay in 2-3-2 position at the time of service.
18.Shift the ball from left-to-right or right-to-left is not permitted.
19.Pushing the ball deliberately is not permitted.
20.The service ball or the rally ball should not touch the antenna.
21.Five Substitutions are allowed for each set.
22.Two time-outs of (30 seconds each) are permitted for each set.
23.The players should wear proper uniform (Jerseys).
24.Numbers from 1-12 should only be used.
